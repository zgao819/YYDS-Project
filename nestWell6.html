Family Safety

Domestic violence refers to violence that occurs between family members who are connected by marriage or intimate relationships, blood ties, and the law. It includes physical, sexual, emotional, and other forms of violence that occur in family relationships, as well as threatening to use violence. Domestic violence may occur between husband and wife, parents and children, brothers and sisters, grandparents and grandchildren and other family members.

Help with mental health and addiction

If you're in immediate danger, call 111 and ask for the police. If it's unsafe to speak, push 55 on a mobile (or any number on a landline) to be put through to the police.

Mental health and addiction services

You do not have to deal with a mental illness or addiction on your own — there are resources, helplines, support groups, websites and counsellors available.
