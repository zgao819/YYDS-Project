Cannabis

Cannabis can have several potential harms, particularly with heavy, frequent, or prolonged use. The risks associated with cannabis are influenced by various factors, including the method of consumption, the potency of THC, individual susceptibility, and the age at which use begins.

CANNABIS AND MARIJUANA (weed)

There is no known safe use of cannabis during pregnancy. Increased social acceptability of its use, the perception that it is safe, and reports that it reduces nausea and depression has increased use. THC the active ingredient in cannabis is stronger now and cannabis is available in a variety of forms such as candies, drinks, suppositories, snacks, lollipops and capsules. This has resulted in more women using it, at a great frequency and at a higher strength.

Using large amounts of cannabis or more frequently can lead to these risks for mothers
  • preeclampsia or pregnancy related high blood pressure
  • placental abruption or placenta coming away from the womb early
  • gestational weight gains greater that recommended for a healthy pregnancy
  • overall poor health
  • anemia at birth
Risks for the baby
  • lower birth weights
  • tmore likely to be referred to the Neonatal Intensive Care Units
  • because mothers may use cannabis in combination with alcohol, tobacco and other drugs there may be affects to early brain development
Not all effects of cannabis and other drug use observed in mothers and babies is due to cannabis use alone. Misuse of maternal cannabis or illicit drugs worldwide is associated with inadequate prenatal care, poor nutrition, poverty, mental illness, a history of sexual or physical abuse.

Children exposed to cannabis and other drugs during pregnancy are also likely to be exposed to a home where there are similar risks. These risks will further impact healthy development and wellbeing.

It is never too late to reduce the risks of cannabis and other drug use for you and your baby, so talk to someone…your whānau, your midwife, or your doctor. For information on where to get further help here are some links.
  • https://www.cads.org.nz/
  • https://www.waitematadhb.govt.nz/hospitals-clinics/clinics-services/cads/
  • https://www.aodcollaborative.org.nz/vdb/document/169
All recreational drugs, including smoking, taken during pregnancy can pass to the baby. Depending on the kinds of drugs used, how much and how often and when they are used, they can harm the baby's brain and organs.
